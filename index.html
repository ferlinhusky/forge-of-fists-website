The Game

What is Forge of Fists?

Forge of Fists is set in the land of Almokon, which is ruled by the old and wise emperor, Manfred the Most Loved. Unfortunately, Manfred is dying (for he is old) and upon his death he knows his empire will crumble (for he is wise).

To maintain peace in Almokon, Manfred must decide, now, who is to wear the crown of the empire upon his passing. You, the player, are one of many monarchs vying for Manfred's favor. Your ability to rule, to protect, and to grow your realm will determine if you will be the next emperor of Almokon.

Features

How It Works

⚔ Alternating Actions

Each turn, one player takes a single action. Then the other responds. No phases, no priority stacks — pure, clean decision-making.

⬛ 40-Card Decks

A tight constraint that rewards craft. Every card must earn its place. Redundancy is a choice; surprise is an art.

◈ 21 Bounty Points

Win by accumulating Bounty Points through card play, contested territory, and fulfilled declarations — not by reducing a life total.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the basics?

Two players, each player selects a Monarch and a Capitol of a matching faction. They each build a 40-card deck around that faction identity.

What are the factions?

Conquest, Community, Creed, Trade and Diplomacy. There are also neutral cards that can be used by any faction.

Am I limited to building a deck with just my faction and neutral cards?

No! The factions are arranged in such a way that each faction has two aligned factions from which a player may include up to 12 aligned faction cards in their deck.

I have my deck, how do I play?

Each player has their own realm and between the realms sits the Outland. Units are played into the Outland and all other card types are played into the realm.

Is this a CCG like Pokémon or Magic?

No, it's a living card game (LCG). You get all the cards.

Well how many cards are there?

Ten monarchs, ten capitols, 20 cards specific to each of the five factions and 51 neutral cards. With three copies of each faction and neutral card, that's 473 cards. Plus some token cards.

What card games inspired Forge of Fists / who did you steal mechanics from?

Deadlands: Doomtown, Star Wars Unlimited, Magic, Quest for the Grail, Dune, Flesh & Blood.
